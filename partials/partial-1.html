The Value of Money

Most economists are in agreement that the inflation in the United States during the past three years has been the worst since the early 1940s, taking account of both severity and duration. But they cannot agree on the nature of the inflatio that is engulfing the American economy. To some, inflation denotes a spectacular rise in consumer prices; to others, an excessive aggregate demand; and to at least one economist, it is the creation of new money by our monetary authorities. This disagreement among economists is more than an academic difference on the meaning of a popular term. A review of some basic principles of economics that are applicable to money may shed light on the problem. Hans F.
